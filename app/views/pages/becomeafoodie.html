Recommendations on foodierecs are based on only the opinions of foodies, and no one else

Why?

There are two kinds of people in the world. The first kind are foodies, let’s call one Jack. The second are non-foodies, let’s call one … Jick.

Jack appreciates great food – he loves discovering the nuances of interesting cuisines, trying out new ways of cooking things, and discovering new ingredients. Jack usually also loves trying drinks with interesting and fun ingredients – in short, he enjoys food that someone has put care and effort into, and that shows.

Jick likes Olive Garden. And not just for the soup-salad-breadsticks combo; he thinks the pasta there is amazing. His favorite area of New York is Times Square. The craziest thing he’s ever tried is a chicken kebab.

What caused Jick to have such faulty taste buds? Is it an innate characteristic? Or the fact his parents gave him such a ridiculous name? Or maybe, they fed him a little too much Kraft Macaroni and Cheese as a kid. Whatever it is, Jick’s restaurant recommendations aren’t to be trusted. They are to be feared.

What does this have to do with anything?

Most restaurant recommendation sites are filled with Jicks. One study* estimates that 99% of the people rating restaurants are Jicks. So when you look at reviews, you’re likely basing your next meal based on what a Jick thinks. Scary, huh?

The good news

On foodierecs, only Jack’s opinion matters. We take what the Jacks of this world like, and use that to make a restaurant recommendation for what you might like. We also take into account what you’ve liked in the past and try to use that to match what Foodie recommendation you might like.

The bad news

We have to assume you are not a Foodie. It’s nothing personal, but you are not to be trusted.

The better news

Ok, so maybe one day we could trust you. One of the following would have to happen: 1. You rate at least 50 fantastic places and tell us what you get to eat and/or drink there. Then, you apply to be a Foodie. The current group of foodies can then see what you like, and vote for/against you. Majority vote wins.
2. You rate at least 10 fantastic places and you get nominated by a current Foodie. We trust our Foodies to find other great Foodies. The current group of foodies can then see what you like and vote for/against you. Majority vote wins.
